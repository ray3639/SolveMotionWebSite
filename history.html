History of Kinesiology & Why Movement Matters

Kinesiology is the study of human movement. It connects anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, neuroscience, and behavior. The goal is to understand how we move, why we move the way we do, and how small changes can affect long-term health.

From curiosity to science

  • Anatomy — how bones and muscles are built.
  • Physiology — how the body responds to effort.
  • Biomechanics — how forces act during movement.
  • Motor control — how the brain coordinates actions.
  • Habits & behavior — how choices stack up over time.

How this connects to Solve Motion

Momentum focuses on how you feel and move. Solvelt focuses on how you think and decide. Both grow out of the same idea: understanding patterns so you can change them.
